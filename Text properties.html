TYPOGRAPHY OF GAMES.

Games are defined by interaction, rules, goals, and a sense of challenge, often involving both mental and physical stimulation.
They encompass a wide range of activities, from traditional board games and sports to digital video games. Games are a fundamental part of human culture, providing entertainment, social interaction, and opportunities for learning and skill development.
Characteristics of Games:

Interaction: Games require player interaction, whether it's with the game environment, other players, or both.

Rules: Games are governed by a set of rules that define how the game is played and what actions are permissible.

Goals: Games have objectives that players strive to achieve, which can range from simple tasks to complex challenges.

Challenge: Games often involve overcoming obstacles or achieving goals despite some level of difficulty.

Types of Games:

Board Games: Traditional games played on a board, often involving strategy and chance.

Sports: Physical games that involve teams or individuals competing against each other.

Video Games: Digital games played on computers or consoles, often featuring immersive graphics and complex narratives.

Role-Playing Games (RPGs): Games where players assume the roles of characters in a fictional setting, often involving storytelling and character development.
